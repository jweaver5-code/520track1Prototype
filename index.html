Hiring match platform — prototype

Static UI sketch: roles, decision points, immutable audit records, user-facing explanations, and human review — without exposing proprietary model weights or prompts.

What this prototype stands for

The product automates which jobs surface for whom and how candidates are ordered for employers, using preferences, experience signals, and employer requirements. The business value is the decision engine; governance makes those outcomes defensible.

  • Actors: job seekers, employers, recruiters, staffing agencies — mapped to platform roles and permissions.
  • Trust: every scored or ranked outcome produces an append-only audit event with inputs, policy version, and outcome — suitable for regulators and internal replay without shipping secret sauce.
  • User-facing layer: plain-language “why you saw this” plus a path to request human review of a specific decision.

Platform roles (≥3)

Admin
Policy packs, model registry pointers, feature flags, compliance exports, escalation queue.
Applicant
Job seeker: profile, resume, preferences, applications, transparency on ranking/matching.
Reviewer / recruiter
Employer or agency user: search, shortlists, human override within policy, review requests.

What you log (conceptually)

  • Decision ID, tenant, actor, timestamp (UTC)
  • Policy + model + feature set version IDs
  • Hashed or redacted inputs; never raw proprietary prompts in user exports
  • Outcome + calibrated explanation template ID
Time (UTC) Type Subject Policy / model Outcome summary

Production systems store these rows in a WORM or append-only store; legal hold and retention follow jurisdiction. This page uses in-memory demo data only.

Risks to monitor

  • Demographic bias in ranking — proxy features (school, zip, name patterns) can skew outcomes; monitor cohort parity and error rates by protected class where lawfully allowed.
  • Filtered view of candidates — personalization can hide qualified people; surface “coverage” metrics to employers and avoid demographic targeting in ranking.

Controls (paired with audit)

  • Pre-deployment bias testing; periodic drift checks.
  • Blinded features for certain stages; opt-in reveal with logging.
  • Employer-facing “why ranked” uses approved templates only.
  • Escalation to human review on disputed or edge-case decisions.

Baseline transparency (without exposing proprietary logic)

Users see categories of factors (e.g., skills overlap, location policy, recency), policy version, and data you provided. They do not see embeddings, weights, or internal prompt text.

Example applicant notice: “This job appeared higher because it matches your saved skills (Python, APIs), matches your location preference, and was posted recently. Policy: job-matching v2026.04. Decision ID: …”

Request human review

Opens a ticket tied to a decision ID; compliance can respond without exposing model internals.

Aligning with AIS-spring
